Occupational Risk Assessment for the Role of Cook

Physical Risks:

  • Cuts and lacerations: Use of knives, slicers, and other sharp utensils.
  • Burns: Handling hot surfaces, hot oil, and other boiling liquids.
  • Falls and slips: Wet or greasy floors in the kitchen.
  • Repetitive strain and poor posture: Lifting heavy objects, prolonged postures, and repetitive movements.

Chemical Risks:

  • Exposure to cleaning products: Use of detergents, disinfectants, and other chemicals.

Preventive Measures

For Cuts and Lacerations:

  • Training and education: Training in the safe use of knives and cutting equipment.
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE): Use of cut-resistant gloves.

For Burns:

  • Heat handling training: Training on the safe handling of pots, pans, and other hot equipment.
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE): Use of thermal gloves and heat-resistant aprons.

For Falls and Slips:

  • Maintenance and cleaning: Immediate cleaning of spills and non-slip flooring.
  • Proper footwear: Provision of non-slip footwear for all cooks.

For Repetitive Strain and Poor Posture:

  • Ergonomics: Ergonomic design of the workspace, adjusting the height of tables and surfaces.
  • Scheduled breaks: Regular breaks to reduce fatigue and repetitive strain.

For Exposure to Cleaning Products:

  • Proper use of chemicals: Clear instructions on the safe use and handling of cleaning products.
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE): Use of gloves and masks when handling chemicals.

For Work Stress:

  • Time management and planning: Efficient organization of work and shifts to avoid overload.
  • Psychological support: Availability of psychological support and employee wellness programs.

For Long and Rotating Shifts:

  • Shift planning: Advance scheduling of shifts to allow proper personal and family time management.
  • Adequate compensation: Provision of incentives and compensatory breaks for prolonged or night shifts.

Functions of the Works Council

The Works Council has various functions, including:

  • Representing the workers in the company.
  • Negotiating collective agreements and working conditions.
  • Monitoring compliance with labor and safety regulations.
  • Intervening in the resolution of labor conflicts.
